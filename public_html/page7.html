CLUB LEVEL - It all starts with members in a club.


DIVISION LEVEL - Each club is geographically clustered together with other clubs to form

a division. Divisions are overseen by a lieutenant governor, who is a representative of the

district board. We are Division 28 in the California-Nevada-Hawaii District and have eight

clubs in Southern Nevada.


DISTRICT LEVEL - A district is made up of divisions. There are currently 49 districts within Kiwanis International. Each district is led by a governor who is elected at the district’s annual convention. Our district includes all of California, Nevada and Hawaii.

INTERNATIONAL LEVEL - The districts make up Kiwanis International. Currently, there are Kiwanis clubs in approximately 80 countries and geographic territories. Each year, delegates at the Kiwanis International convention elect members and officers of the Kiwanis International Board, which includes a president, president-elect, vice president, past president and 15 trustees

KIWANIS CHILDREN’S FUND - Kiwanis Children's Fund helps by amplifying our impact in communities around the world. They provide grants for Kiwanis Service Leadership Programs, club and district service projects, disaster relief, and for our Worldwide Service Projects - the elimination of maternal and neonatal tetanus and iodine deficiency disorders.

Kiwanis International is a global community of clubs, members and partners dedicated to improving the lives of children one community at a time. Today, we stand with more than 550,000 members - from K-Kids to Key Club to Kiwanis and many ages in between - in 80 countries and geographic areas. Each community has different needs, and Kiwanis empowers members to pursue creative ways to serve the needs of children, such as fighting hunger, improving literacy and offering guidance.

Kiwanis clubs host nearly 150,000 service projects each year.
